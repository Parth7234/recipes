Panneer Tikka Masala

Description

Creating the perfect Paneer Tikka Masala involves a delightful two-part process. First, cubes of fresh paneer are marinated in a thick blend of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and a symphony of spices like turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. These marinated cubes are then pan-fried, grilled, or baked until they are beautifully golden and slightly charred at the edges. Simultaneously, a luscious gravy is prepared by sautéing a base of pureed onions and tomatoes with aromatics until fragrant. A medley of spices is then added and cooked until the oil separates, after which a rich paste of cashews or a splash of cream is stirred in to create a velvety smooth texture, often finished with a touch of crushed kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). Finally, the charred paneer tikka pieces are gently folded into this vibrant, creamy gravy, simmered for a few minutes to absorb the flavors, and garnished with fresh coriander for a restaurant-worthy finish.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup butter
  • 1 pound paneer, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium jalapeño peppers, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashews
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 pint half-and-half
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add paneer cubes; cook and stir until golden, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add onions, bell pepper, jalapeños, ground cashews, garlic paste, ginger paste, cayenne pepper, cumin, coriander, and garam masala; cook and stir until well combined and fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Mix tomato sauce, half-and-half, and salt into paneer mixture; simmer until thickened, about 30 minutes.
